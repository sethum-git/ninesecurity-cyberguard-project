Privacy Policy

How we protect and handle your data

Last Updated: February 11, 2026

Introduction

At CyberGuard ("we," "our," or "us"), we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our threat intelligence platform and related services.

Our Commitment: We comply with GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable privacy laws and regulations.

Information We Collect

1. Personal Information

  • Account Information: Name, email address, job title, company name
  • Contact Information: Work email, phone number, business address
  • Authentication Data: Username, encrypted password, multi-factor authentication details
  • Billing Information: Payment method details, billing address (processed securely by third-party providers)

2. Threat Intelligence Data

  • Security Events: IP addresses, threat indicators, incident reports
  • System Logs: Access logs, activity data, security alerts
  • Analytics: Usage patterns, threat trends, attack vectors

3. Technical Data

  • Device Information: IP address, browser type, operating system
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, features used, time spent
  • Cookies: Essential and functional cookies for platform operation

Purpose Legal Basis
Provide threat intelligence services Contract performance

How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information with:

  • Service Providers: Cloud infrastructure, payment processing, customer support
  • Security Partners: Threat intelligence sharing (anonymized data only)
  • Legal Authorities: When required by law or to protect rights

Data Security

We implement industry-leading security measures:

  • AES-256 encryption for data at rest
  • TLS 1.3 for data in transit
  • 24/7 security monitoring and threat detection
  • Regular penetration testing and security audits
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) support

Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: Request copies of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured format
  • Right to Object: Object to certain types of processing

To exercise your rights, contact us at privacy@cyberguard.com

International Data Transfers

We operate globally and may transfer data to countries with different data protection laws. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place through:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • EU-US Data Privacy Framework compliance
  • Binding Corporate Rules

Children's Privacy

Our services are not intended for individuals under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us immediately.

Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide you with our services
  • Comply with legal obligations (up to 7 years)
  • Resolve disputes and enforce agreements

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We will notify you of material changes through:

  • Email notification
  • In-platform notice
  • Website banner
